Week 4

3 Quotes

“Instead, we focused on using appropriate, often low-tech, means to simulate and test the experiences the proposals would engender – their aesthetics, their social effects, and their cultural implications – with the older people in the three communities. ”

Different types of prototypes functions differently, we should always choose the appropriate prototypes which benefit the design research process to figure out specific problems and clarify goals. In this case, low- tech prototypes are efficient.

“Despite the almost complete failure of the test – we received only two meaningful answers from the hundreds of calls we placed – we were paradoxically enthused by the experience. ”

It is common that our design experience might face unexpected failure. But we can still learn from the failures and avoid the problems in iterations.

“ Beyond this, however, the situation in the Bijlmer presented us with more complex and richer issues than the other sites, and it was the success of the ideas to raise these social and cultural issues that convinced us to pursue the system further. ”

It is intersting that from this "unqualified success", people learned a lot. Like the reason for success is lacking of technical failures, which are always big challenges for designers to overcome. Also, it provided topics and environments for both the older group and other inhabitants to engage with and this proved future display in public area was an effective way to communicate in the local community.
